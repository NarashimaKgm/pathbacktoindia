Aadhaar Card

Last updated: February 2026

Aadhaar is a 12-digit unique identity number issued by UIDAI (Unique Identification Authority of India). It is linked to your biometrics — fingerprints and iris scan. You will need it for almost everything in India: opening a bank account, getting a SIM card, filing taxes, receiving government benefits, and more. Getting it sorted early makes every other step easier.

Note

If you already have an Aadhaar from before you moved to the US, you don't need a new one. But you may need to update your address and phone number. You can do this online at myaadhaar.uidai.gov.in or by visiting an Aadhaar enrollment center.

Documents you'll need

For a fresh Aadhaar enrollment, you need proof of identity and proof of address. Commonly accepted documents:

  • Proof of identity — Indian passport, PAN card, or voter ID.
  • Proof of address — Passport with current Indian address, utility bill, bank statement, rental agreement, or a letter from a gazetted officer / tehsildar. If you've just arrived and don't have address proof in your name, a landlord's NOC or a relative's address proof along with a letter confirming you live there can work.
  • Date of birth proof — Passport or birth certificate.

Tip

Carry originals of all documents. The operator will scan them at the center. Photocopies are not enough on their own.

Enrollment process

  1. Book an appointment — Visit uidai.gov.in and book a slot at a nearby Aadhaar enrollment center. Walk-ins are possible but expect long wait times.
  2. Visit the center — Bring your original documents. The operator will verify them, take your photograph, scan your fingerprints, and capture an iris scan.
  3. Collect your acknowledgment slip — You'll receive an enrollment number. Use this to track the status of your application online.
  4. Receive your Aadhaar — The physical card is mailed to your address in 2-4 weeks. You can download the e-Aadhaar (digital copy) from the UIDAI website as soon as it's generated, usually within 2-3 weeks.

Common issues

  • Address proof difficulties — If you're staying with family and don't have any utility bills in your name, the head of household can provide their Aadhaar along with a consent letter. Some centers are more flexible than others — if one center rejects your documents, try another.
  • Biometric failures — If your fingerprints don't scan well (common if your hands are dry or worn), the operator may retry a few times. Iris scan is the fallback. Moisturizing your hands before the appointment can help.
  • Long processing times — While the official timeline is 2-3 weeks, delays do happen. Download the e-Aadhaar as soon as it's available — you don't need to wait for the physical card to start using it.

Warning

Never share your Aadhaar number or OTP with anyone over the phone. Aadhaar-related fraud is common. UIDAI will never call you asking for your Aadhaar details.
