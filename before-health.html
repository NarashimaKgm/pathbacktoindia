Health Insurance

Last updated: February 2026

Health insurance is one of the easiest things to underestimate during relocation planning. A short uninsured gap can become expensive fast if an emergency happens before your move to India. Plan this timeline before you choose your final working day.

When employer coverage ends

There is no universal rule that every plan follows. In many companies, coverage ends either on your last day of employment or at month-end of your last working month. The exact date depends on your employer's health plan document and HR policy.

  • Ask HR for the exact termination date in writing for medical, dental, and vision coverage.
  • Confirm dependent coverage timing so family members are not left uninsured.
  • Ask when your COBRA election packet will be sent and where it will be mailed or emailed.

Warning

Do not assume coverage continues to month-end. Confirm your actual plan rule before setting your resignation date or flight date.

COBRA after resignation or layoff

If your employer-sponsored coverage ends because of resignation or layoff, COBRA usually lets you continue the same group health plan for a limited period.

  • COBRA generally applies to private-sector and state/local government employers with 20 or more employees.
  • You typically get 60 days to elect COBRA from the later of the date coverage is lost or the date the election notice is provided.
  • After you elect COBRA, you have 45 days to make the first premium payment.
  • Initial COBRA premiums can be high because you usually pay the full premium plus up to a 2% administrative fee.
  • Coverage is generally retroactive to the date your employer coverage ended if you elect and pay within timelines.

Tip

If cash flow is tight, remember the COBRA 45-day initial payment window. You can still preserve uninterrupted coverage as long as you elect and pay on time.

Layoff severance and employer-paid coverage

When layoffs happen, some employers subsidize COBRA premiums for a few months as part of severance. This is common, but not legally required under federal COBRA rules.

  • Read your severance agreement carefully for exact duration and what is paid (medical only vs medical+dental+vision).
  • Confirm whether subsidy ends immediately if you get a new job with benefits.
  • Track subsidy end date now so you do not miss your next coverage transition.

Alternatives to COBRA

COBRA is not your only option. Losing job-based coverage also qualifies you for a Special Enrollment Period in the Health Insurance Marketplace.

  • Marketplace SEP: generally available from 60 days before to 60 days after loss of qualifying coverage.
  • Spouse's employer plan: loss of your own coverage is usually a qualifying life event for mid-year enrollment.
  • Medicaid/CHIP: if household eligibility changes after job loss, this may be available.

Note

If your move to India date is close, compare total cost vs convenience. Sometimes COBRA for a short bridge period is simpler even if monthly cost is higher.

Practical checklist before your last day

  1. Ask HR for your exact benefits termination date.
  2. Get COBRA administrator contact details and expected notice date.
  3. Estimate COBRA premium and decide your backup option (Marketplace/spouse plan).
  4. Refill essential prescriptions and schedule pending appointments while still covered.
  5. Keep digital copies of insurance cards, claims, and EOBs before access changes.
  6. Do not leave any uninsured gap between resignation and travel to India.
