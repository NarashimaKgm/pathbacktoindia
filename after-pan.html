PAN Card

Last updated: February 2026

PAN (Permanent Account Number) is a 10-character alphanumeric identifier issued by the Income Tax Department of India. It is mandatory for filing income tax returns, opening bank accounts, making investments, and any financial transaction above a certain threshold. If you're going to live and earn in India, you need one.

Note

If you already have a PAN card from before you left India, you don't need a new one - PAN numbers are permanent and don't expire. You may need to update your address or name if they've changed. You can do this through the NSDL or UTIITSL portals.

New PAN application

If you've never had a PAN, you can apply online through one of two authorized portals:

  • NSDL (Protean) - Apply at onlineservices.nsdl.com using Form 49A (for Indian citizens). Fee is Rs 107.
  • UTIITSL - Apply at pan.utiitsl.com. Similar process and fees.

Documents required

  • Proof of identity - Aadhaar card, Indian passport, or voter ID.
  • Proof of address - Aadhaar, passport, utility bill, or bank statement.
  • Proof of date of birth - Aadhaar, passport, or birth certificate.
  • Passport-size photograph - Recent photo with white background.

Tip

If you have Aadhaar, the fastest route is the instant e-PAN facility on the Income Tax portal (incometax.gov.in). It's free and you can get your PAN allotted in minutes - no documents to upload, as it pulls everything from your Aadhaar. This only works for first-time PAN applicants with a valid Aadhaar.

Linking PAN with Aadhaar

PAN must be linked to Aadhaar - this is mandatory. If not linked, your PAN becomes inoperative and you cannot file taxes or use it for financial transactions.

  • Link online at incometax.gov.in under "Link Aadhaar."
  • A late linking fee of Rs 1,000 applies if you've missed earlier deadlines. The government periodically extends deadlines, so check the current status.
  • Make sure the name and date of birth match exactly on both PAN and Aadhaar. Mismatches will cause the linking to fail - you'll need to correct one of them first.

Updating PAN details

If your name, address, or other details have changed since your PAN was issued:

  • Apply for a PAN correction/update through the NSDL or UTIITSL portals.
  • You'll need supporting documents for whatever you're changing (e.g., passport for name change, utility bill for address change).
  • The updated PAN card is mailed to your registered address. The e-PAN is available for download sooner.

Warning

Having more than one PAN is illegal in India and can attract a penalty of Rs 10,000. If you were allotted multiple PANs by mistake (it happens), surrender the extra one through the NSDL or UTIITSL portal.
