Bucket List

Last updated: February 2026

You've probably been meaning to visit certain places in the US for years - national parks, cities, landmarks, restaurants, or experiences you kept pushing to "next year." Now is the time. Once you move back to India, returning to the US as a tourist is not straightforward, and it could be years before you get the chance again.

Warning

If you don't already have a B1/B2 (tourist) visa, getting one after you move back can be a long wait. US tourist visa appointment slots in India often have wait times of several months to over a year, and having an appointment doesn't guarantee approval.

Make the list now

Sit down and write out every place or experience you've been putting off. Don't filter - just get it all out. Then prioritize based on what matters most to you and what's realistic given your timeline.

Some ideas to jog your memory:

  • National parks - Grand Canyon, Yellowstone, Yosemite, Zion, Glacier, Acadia, Denali (Alaska). These are genuinely hard to experience from anywhere else in the world.
  • Cities you've never visited - New York, San Francisco, Chicago, New Orleans, Nashville, Seattle, Boston. Each has a character you won't find elsewhere.
  • Road trips - Pacific Coast Highway, Route 66, Blue Ridge Parkway. The open-road experience is uniquely American.
  • Seasonal experiences - Fall foliage in New England, cherry blossoms in DC, skiing in Colorado, beach weekends in Florida or Hawaii.
  • Food and culture - That barbecue place in Texas, the seafood shack in Maine, the jazz club in New Orleans, the museum you never got around to.
  • Events - A baseball or football game, a music festival, Mardi Gras, Fourth of July fireworks in a new city.

Practical tips

  • Start early - Don't wait until your last month. Some trips need advance booking, and your final weeks will be consumed by packing, paperwork, and farewells.
  • Use your remaining PTO - If you're still employed and have unused vacation days, this is the best use of them. Some employers also offer a notice period where you can take time off.
  • Travel light - You're going to be shipping or selling your belongings soon. This is a good time to practice traveling with less.
  • Bring friends or family along - If your parents or family are visiting from India before the move, take them to these places too. It becomes a shared memory.

Tip

If a big trip isn't feasible, even smaller things count - a favorite local hike, a restaurant you've always wanted to try, a day trip to a nearby town. The point is to not leave with a list of regrets about things that were an hour's drive away.

Things you'll miss more than you expect

Beyond the bucket list destinations, there are everyday American experiences that are hard to replicate in India. Consider doing some of these one last time:

  • A lazy weekend at a farmer's market
  • Hiking trails near your home
  • Your favorite coffee shop or diner
  • A drive through quiet suburban streets at sunset
  • Grocery shopping at Trader Joe's or Costco
  • A cookout or potluck with friends

These sound small, but they're the textures of daily life that you'll remember the most. Don't wait until the last day to notice them.
